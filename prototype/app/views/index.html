Funding stream 'Hub & Spoke' concept prototypes

Starting with the Adult skills fund, we've developed an approach where funding streams have a dedicated 'Hub' page with an overview of allocation and links to statements, schedules, and payment history.

Version Date Status Reasoning Actions
Version 4.1 26 January 2026 Tested & iterated

In this version, we have:

  • Added a funding stream / funding period 'Hub' page, containing high level overview of allocation and links to related allocation statement, payment schedule and payment history.
  • Displaying 'inactive' cards on 'home' page for payment schedule and payment history pages before the data becomes available.
  • Added a funding stream specific payment schedule pages. Pulling the payment schedules out of contracts into a dedicated page as downloadable .CSVs including previously released versions.
  • Added a funding stream specific payment history page which pulls through and displays the funding stream specific payment history from the Calculate Funding Service (CFS) and displays it on a single page.

This version was taken through user research round 2 (Jan 2026) - see findings deck here

Data driven allocation statements

Starting with the Adult skills fund, we've tested and iterated a design pattern for handling data-driven allocation statements, allowing internal staff to create digital allocation statements in an automated way.

Minimum valuable product (private Beta)
Version Date Status Reasoning Actions
MVP 11 November 2025 Tested and iterated The minimum valuable product for the data-driven allocation statement is informed by 3 rounds of user testing across alpha and private beta.
  • designed as a pattern to be scaled out across Funding Service grants and funding streams, and used across the DfE to communicate funding information to end users
  • data-driven using funding allocation data from the Calculate Funding Service
  • private beta scoped to deliver adult funding allocations release for academic year 2026-2027 Advanced learner loans and Adult skills fund
